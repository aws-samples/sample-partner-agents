Meeting Notes — Discovery Call with Acme Manufacturing
Date: May 18, 2026
Attendees: Sarah Patel (VP Engineering, Acme), Tom Vargas (IT Director, Acme), Ming Zhu (AWS Partner SA)

Background:
This was an initial discovery call. Acme Manufacturing reached out via our website contact form
after seeing a competitor case study. They run an aging on-prem ERP system and are starting to
evaluate cloud options for the first time. They are at the very beginning of their journey and
have not yet made any decisions.

Discussion Highlights:
- Sarah described their current pain: 4-hour batch jobs that run overnight, frequent hardware failures,
  $80K/year in VMware licenses up for renewal in Q1 2027.
- Tom mentioned the board has asked them to "explore cloud" but hasn't given them a budget or timeline yet.
- Acme has zero AWS experience — no AWS account, no AWS-skilled engineers on staff.
- They asked basic questions: "what is the difference between AWS and Azure?", "how does AWS pricing work?",
  "can we move our SAP system?".
- Sarah is the technical lead but not the decision maker. The CFO will sign off on any cloud spend.
- They are also talking to a Microsoft Azure rep next week.

Customer's Current State:
- 200 employees, $50M annual revenue, mid-market manufacturing
- All workloads on-prem (1 small data center in Ohio)
- Critical systems: SAP ERP, custom-built MES (manufacturing execution system), file shares, email
- IT team of 4 — Sarah, Tom, and 2 sysadmins. No cloud experience.

What Acme Needs Next:
- High-level overview of AWS for manufacturing
- Pricing/cost comparison vs. on-prem and vs. Azure
- Reference customers in their industry
- Help building the business case for the CFO
- Roadmap for getting started — they don't know what step 1 looks like

Action Items Discussed:
- Sarah will share a network diagram and infrastructure list with us by end of week
- We agreed to schedule a follow-up technical deep-dive in 2 weeks
- Sarah asked for the AWS Well-Architected Framework whitepaper

Sentiment: Cautiously interested. No commitment yet. They could easily go to Azure if we don't move fast.
