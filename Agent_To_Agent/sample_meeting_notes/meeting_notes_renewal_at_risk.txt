Meeting Notes — Renewal Risk Discussion with GreenLogistics
Date: May 18, 2026
Attendees: Robert Yang (CIO, GreenLogistics), Lisa Brand (Director of Cloud Operations, GreenLogistics),
            Ming Zhu (AWS Partner SA), Tuan Vo (AWS Partner SA)

Background:
GreenLogistics is an existing AWS customer (3-year customer, ~$60K/month spend). Their commitment
agreement comes up for renewal in 90 days. We requested this meeting after our automated retention
analytics flagged the account as "at-risk." Robert was direct: they are seriously considering moving
to Azure for their next term. This meeting was a candid conversation about what's gone wrong and
what we'd need to do to keep their business.

What We Heard (the bad news):

Issue 1 — Cost overruns:
- Their AWS bill grew 40% YoY without a corresponding increase in workload.
- Robert: "I keep getting calls from my CFO asking why our cloud bill is so high."
- Lisa pointed out specific issues: orphaned EBS volumes, oversized RDS instances, idle NAT gateways.
- They feel they have not been getting proactive cost optimization advice from us.

Issue 2 — Slow support response:
- Two P2 support tickets from last quarter took >24 hours for first response.
- They are on Business Support tier and feel they should be getting Enterprise-level responsiveness.
- Robert mentioned that Azure has offered them a dedicated Customer Success Manager included
  with their tier.

Issue 3 — Missed feature opportunities:
- They have been running their own Kubernetes cluster on EC2 instead of EKS (and didn't know about
  EKS Auto Mode until Lisa stumbled across it).
- They are still using S3 standard for archival data that should be in Glacier — would save them
  ~$8K/month.
- They're using self-managed PostgreSQL on EC2 instead of Aurora.
- Robert: "We feel like we're paying for a Ferrari but driving a Honda."

Issue 4 — Competitive pressure:
- Microsoft has offered them a 20% discount on equivalent Azure services for a 3-year commitment.
- Microsoft is also offering free migration consulting and a dedicated TAM.
- Robert is meeting Microsoft's enterprise team next week.

What They Want from Us (to retain):

1. A formal cost optimization assessment — they want a detailed report showing where they can save
   money. Lisa asked specifically for a Trusted Advisor review and a Compute Optimizer analysis.
2. Migration plan for their self-managed services to AWS-managed equivalents (EKS, Aurora, Glacier).
3. An Enterprise Support evaluation — they want to know what they'd get for the upgrade cost.
4. Executive escalation to a senior AWS person (VP-level) to demonstrate AWS' commitment to them.
5. A multi-year renewal proposal with built-in cost guarantees and committed savings.
6. A dedicated AWS Solutions Architect for their account.

Robert's Quote: "We're not in love with Azure. But you guys have been on autopilot. We need to see
that AWS still wants our business."

Decisions Made:
- Tuan committed to escalating internally. AWS will engage a Senior Account Manager and Solutions Architect.
- Lisa will share their current architecture and 12 months of usage data by Wednesday.
- We will deliver a cost optimization report and modernization roadmap within 2 weeks.
- Renewal decision deadline: 90 days from today.

Stage: At Risk — Closed Lost is a real possibility
Renewal Value: $720K/year ($60K/mo) — losing this account would hurt our retention metrics
Time-Sensitive: Microsoft's offer expires June 15

Customer Sentiment: Frustrated but not gone. They want to stay if we step up. The next 30 days
are critical.
