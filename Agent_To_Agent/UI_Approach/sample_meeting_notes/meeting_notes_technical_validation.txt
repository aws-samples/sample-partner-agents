Meeting Notes — Technical Architecture Review with FinSecure Bank
Date: May 18, 2026
Attendees: Daniel Kim (Chief Architect, FinSecure), Priya Shah (Head of Security, FinSecure),
            Marco Lopez (Cloud Lead, FinSecure), Ming Zhu (AWS Partner SA), Chidu Muthappan (AWS Partner SA)

Background:
This was the third meeting with FinSecure Bank, a regional financial services company.
The deal is in technical validation stage. Stakeholders have endorsed the migration in principle
but the architecture and security teams need to validate that AWS meets their compliance bar
before signing the SOW. FinSecure operates under SOC 2 Type II, PCI-DSS, and SEC 17a-4 requirements.

Discussion Highlights:

Security Team Concerns (raised by Priya):
- Concern about data residency: their regulator requires customer financial records to remain in US-East
  region only. They want a written commitment.
- Asked specifically about AWS KMS — whether they can use customer-managed keys (CMKs) and if there's
  any scenario where AWS engineers can decrypt their data.
- Wants a copy of the latest SOC 2 Type II report from AWS Artifact.
- Asked about CloudTrail log immutability — requirement is 7 years of tamper-evident audit logs.
- Pushed back on multi-tenant services (RDS, S3) — wants assurance about isolation.

Architecture Team Concerns (raised by Daniel):
- Their core trading platform requires sub-millisecond latency between application and database.
  Asked if Aurora can meet this. We discussed RDS Proxy and read replicas.
- Want to validate failover RPO/RTO — current spec is RPO < 60 seconds, RTO < 5 minutes.
- Asked if we can do a Well-Architected Review focused on the Reliability and Security pillars.
- Marco suggested running a 30-day proof-of-concept on a non-critical workload (their reporting system)
  before committing to the trading platform migration.

Decisions Made:
- FinSecure agreed to schedule a Well-Architected Review for the second week of June.
- Priya will send us their compliance requirements doc (CRD-2026-04) by Friday.
- They want a custom security architecture document covering KMS, CloudTrail, GuardDuty, and Macie
  before any contract is signed.
- AWS to provide the latest SOC 2 Type II from Artifact.
- 30-day POC on the reporting workload approved in principle, contracts pending.

Open Questions / Blockers:
- Their general counsel needs to review the AWS Customer Agreement before signing.
- Procurement asked for AWS' Business Associate Agreement template (not a HIPAA shop, but precautionary).
- Daniel wants comparable benchmark data showing Aurora vs. their current Oracle DB latency.

Customer Sentiment: Engaged but cautious. They want to migrate but won't move until security and
compliance teams sign off.

Expected Next Steps from Customer Side:
- Daniel will send architecture diagrams of trading platform by next Tuesday
- Priya will deliver compliance requirements doc by Friday
- Marco will scope the POC workload (10 servers, ~2 TB data)

Stage: Technical Validation
Anticipated Close: End of Q3 2026 if WAR and security review go well
Estimated Monthly Spend: $45K (POC) → $180K (full production)
